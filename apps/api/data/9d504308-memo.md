## 3. Precedent analysis

Aave most closely resembles Maker/Sky in its trajectory: early mover, survived multiple cycles, launched a stablecoin (GHO vs DAI), and is now focused on governance optimization and revenue capture. Maker's DAI grew from $500M to $5B+ over several years after launch. GHO is at $564M and growing faster in relative terms.

The compound comparison is also relevant. Compound was Aave's primary competitor until 2022. Compound V3 now sits at $1.8B TVL vs Aave's $26.6B, a 15:1 ratio [DefiLlama, 2026-03-07]. Compound's decline wasn't caused by a technical failure but by slower governance, slower multi-chain expansion, and less aggressive product iteration. This precedent validates the importance of governance activism in maintaining protocol dominance.

## 5. Fundamental analysis

Revenue and fees

Aave generates revenue through borrowing spreads. Borrowers pay interest to depositors, and a portion of that spread accrues to the protocol treasury and token holders through various mechanisms. With $16.4B in active borrows [DefiLlama, 2026-03-07], even a modest average spread generates substantial annualized revenue.

Tokenomics

AAVE has a fixed max supply of 16M tokens [CoinGecko, 2026-03-07]. Circulating supply is 15.18M (94.9% of max), meaning dilution risk is minimal. The community is actively discussing an institutional-grade buyback program [Discourse, 2026-03-07], which would create direct demand for the token from protocol revenue.

The safety module currently uses AAVE staking to backstop protocol risk. A recent governance proposal seeks to reduce safety module emissions [Discourse, 2026-03-07], which would decrease sell pressure from staking rewards.

GHO stablecoin

GHO is Aave's CDP-style stablecoin with $564M in circulation [DefiLlama, 2026-03-07]. It grew from $527M the prior week and $525M the prior month, representing roughly 7% weekly growth. GHO generates revenue for the protocol through borrowing interest, and its growth directly increases Aave's revenue without requiring additional TVL. This is the single most important growth vector for the protocol.

## 9. Technical architecture

Aave V3 is the current production version, featuring efficiency mode (eMode) for correlated assets, isolation mode for new collateral types, and portal functionality for cross-chain liquidity. The protocol's smart contracts are verified and have been audited extensively.

The safety module provides a backstop against bad debt through AAVE token staking. The current governance discussion around reducing emissions suggests the protocol is optimizing this mechanism for long-term sustainability.

GHO is implemented as a facilitator-based model where approved entities can mint GHO against Aave collateral. This design is more flexible than Maker's vault model and allows for future expansion of minting sources.
